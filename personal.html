Personal Projects

Stardust

Animated Short

In Spring of 2022, I took a class on animated media, which covered a history of animation production & technique. For our final project, my class friends, Maryam Ghanem & Julia Howell, & I took this project to the next level. Together, we developed, wrote, illustrated, animated, & recorded audio to produce a pilot episode for our original show: Stardust.

Stardust Episode Intro

Together, we brainstormed lore, characters, setting, & plot. In the production stage, we had a more specified approach to task delegation. Julia managed script-writing, I served as visual lead, & Maryam unified our deliverables through video editing, sound, & effects.

Illustrated with Procreate app on iPad

Animator on TikTok


I love animation––cartoons & fan-imations alike. If you aren't familiar with this genre, fan-imations are animated shorts, based on an existing show/graphic novel, made by fans. After finishing My Hero Academia this past summer, I tried my hand at fan-imation. It was sucessful. Like, really successful. My first ever Tiktok broke 185k views and 45k likes.

Animated using Procreate app on iPad

I continued to make animations up until the school year started. If you haven't seen My Hero Academia, I can't guarantee you will understand what's going on. That said, my videos resonated with the Tiktok fanbase. Feel free to check out my account here

Replicating Style

My ability to emulate and colormatch other art styles comes from years of mimicing the styles of the characters and media I love. I remember lying on the floor as a kid, surrounded by my Pokémon cards trying to make posters of my favorites.

The more I drew, the more trained my eyes became. As an adult, I still love recreating the art that speaks to me. Below is a comparison between a screenshot from Steven Universe versus my freehand drawing.

Sculpture & Woodwork

This past fall, I took an introductury sculpture class. This course taught me to visualize and implement spatial design through modeling, carving, fabricating, casting, fastening, and woodworking. I was trained on various power tools including circular saws, table saws, planars, bandsaws, and sanders.

The photos below show a table I made using recycled wood. The process required significant preliminary effort to salvage, prepare, and level the originally warped, discolored, and splitting wood (not pictured). The final product resides in my dining room and comfortably seats four people.
